can an AI legal assistant claim "clean to file" while hiding a fabricated citation?

An assistant asserts the citations behind a filing and a gate decision. This verifier re-derives that decision from committed evidence — resolving every cite against a rooted court-record corpus and screening each quoted holding for fabrication. A producer cannot claim approval while hiding a fabricated or misquoted authority: the recompute disagrees and the gate fails closed.

The rule

ROOTED

The cite resolves and the quoted holding is found, verbatim, in the rooted record.

MISQUOTE

The cite resolves, but the quote is not in the record — real source, fabricated or inverted quote.

UNRESOLVED

The cite is absent from the rooted corpus — a possible fabrication. Default-deny.

Three ways to cheat — three ways it fails closed

REDERIVATION_MISMATCH

Doctor the claimed verdict (e.g. claim AUTO_APPROVE) and reseal its hash. The recompute yields the honest verdict; the auditor-anchored exact comparator disagrees. (Try the buttons above.)

BAD_FILE_SHA

Doctor the evidence — flip a holding in the corpus — without updating the manifest. The per-file SHA walk catches the byte change.

AnchorViolation

Ship a weaker spec (a looser comparator) in the bundle. Its hash isn't the one the auditor anchored out-of-band → not authoritative → refused.

What this proves — and what it doesn't

✓ Proves

Given this corpus, the gate decision is re-derivable and tamper-evident under a rule the auditor pins and the producer cannot weaken. The thing that produces the answer is not the thing that checks it.

✗ Does not prove

That the corpus records are genuine court records. That's a trust-root question — a verifier can't establish an external fact on its own. In production the fixture corpus is replaced by a trust-root resolver against a rooted authority (CourtListener / PACER).

In one line: the gate grounds citations against a rooted corpus and the verifier attests the gate ran and re-derives — it does not, on its own, establish that the corpus is genuine.
