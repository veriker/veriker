# No-Relicense Pledge

_Dated 2026-06-16._

Veriker is released under the Apache License 2.0. This document is a plain,
public commitment about what that means and what it will keep meaning.

## The pledge

1. **Released code stays Apache-2.0.** Every version of Veriker we have
   published under Apache-2.0 is, and will remain, Apache-2.0. We will not
   retroactively relicense a released version, withdraw it, or move it behind
   a more restrictive license.

2. **The verification engine stays open.** The verifier, the bundle format,
   the manifest schema, the producer SDK, and the generic pilots are the open
   substrate. We will not move the engine to a closed or source-available
   license to drive upgrades.

3. **The open tool is not a funnel.** Veriker does real work standalone, with
   no account, no network call at audit time, and no paid dependency. We will
   not cripple it to sell something else, and we will not add a feature only
   to gate it.

4. **No silent telemetry, ever.** Veriker does not phone home. If any
   telemetry is ever added, it will be opt-in, off by default, and documented
   exactly.

5. **No copyright-assignment CLA.** Contributions are accepted under the
   Developer Certificate of Origin (see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)). We
   do not ask contributors to assign copyright — which is, deliberately, the
   legal precondition for the relicense we are pledging never to do.

## What we do sell — stated plainly

We also build NEXIVERIFY, a commercial audit-trail product for regulated
teams. It monetizes a *layer around* verification — hosting, retention,
trusted-time and cross-organization attestation, conformance evidence, and
support. It never monetizes the verdict itself: the open verifier always
produces the same answer, offline, for free. If that ever stops being true,
this pledge has been broken, and you should hold us to it.
